# SprouT MakerBox Safety Guidelines

Before you start building, programming, and experimenting, please read through these essential safety guidelines to keep both yourself and your hardware safe.

---

## 1. Electrical & Power Safety
While the SprouT MakerBox uses safe, low-voltage power (typically 5V via USB or safe batteries), basic electrical precautions should always be followed:

* **Power Off Before Wiring:** Always disconnect the USB cable or power source from your SprouT board before adding, removing, or changing any components or jumper wires.
* **Avoid Short Circuits:** Never let exposed metal pins, wires, or conductive objects touch each other while the board is powered. This can overheat your board and damage the components.
* **Use Approved Power Sources Only:** Power your SprouT board using a standard computer USB port, a certified USB power adapter, or the recommended battery pack. Never connect the kit components to mains power (wall outlets).
* **If it Feels Hot, Disconnect Immediately!** If any component, sensor, or chip feels unusually hot to the touch, smells like it is burning, or begins to smoke, unplug the USB cable immediately and let it cool down before inspecting.

---

## 2. Safe Hardware Handling
Our plug-and-play sensor and output modules are sturdy, but they contain sensitive electronic circuitry:

* **Hold by the Edges:** When picking up boards or sensors (like the LCD, DHT11, or Bluetooth module), try to hold them by the outer edges of the PCB. Avoid pressing directly on delicate exposed copper pins, solder points, or microchips.
* **Keep Liquids Away:** Work in a completely dry area. Keep water, juices, and other liquids far away from your SprouT MakerBox. *Exception:* If you are doing an experiment with soil moisture, handle the water with extreme care and keep it strictly confined to the sensor tip.
* **Do Not Force Connections:** If a cable, wire header, or module pin does not slide easily into a port or breadboard, do not force it. Double-check the orientation and try again gently.

---

## 3. Component-Specific Precautions
Certain sensors and actuators require extra care during use:

* **Ultrasonic & Sound Sensors:** Do not place these sensors directly up against your ears or eyes. 
* **DC Motor & Moving Parts:** Keep loose hair, clothing, fingers, and jewelry clear of the DC motor's shaft or any attached propellers/wheels while it is running to prevent tangling.
* **Relay Module:** The 1-Channel Relay is designed to switch external loads. **Never** attempt to connect household AC main appliances to the relay. Stick to low-voltage, battery-powered projects (such as 5V/9V motors or LED strips).
