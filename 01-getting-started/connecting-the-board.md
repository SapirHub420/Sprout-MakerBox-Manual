# How to Connect Your SprouT Board

Before you can upload code and make your projects come to life, you need to properly connect your SprouT board to your computer. Follow these simple steps to get plugged in safely!

---

## What You Need
1. Your **SprouT Base Board** (with your chosen controller microchip attached on top).
2. The provided **USB Data Cable**.
3. A **Computer** or Laptop.

---

## Step-by-Step Connection Guide

### 1️⃣ Step 1: Inspect Your Workspace
Make sure your table is completely **clean, dry, and free of any metallic objects** (like coins, paperclips, or stray wires) that could touch the bottom of your board.

### 2️⃣ Step 2: Plug Cable Into the SprouT Board
Take the small end of your USB cable and gently plug it into the USB port on your SprouT board. 
> ⚠️ **Note:** Do not force it! If it doesn't slide in smoothly, flip the cable over and try again.

### 3️⃣ Step 3: Connect to Your Computer
Plug the large, standard end of the USB cable into an open USB port on your computer. 

### 4️⃣ Step 4: Check for Power (The Indicator Lights)
Look closely at your SprouT board. As soon as you plug it into your computer:
* A small **LED power light** on the board should turn on (usually red or green).
* This tells you that electricity is flowing safely from your computer to your SprouT kit!

---

## Important Connection Rules

* **Always Unplug to Wire:** Whenever you are connecting new sensors (like the Light Sensor or Ultrasonic Sensor) or output modules (like the Buzzer or LCD screen), **always unplug the USB cable from your computer first**. Once you finish wiring, plug it back in!
* **Use the Right Cable:** Always use the USB cable that came inside your SprouT MakerBox. Some phone charging cables only transmit power and cannot carry code data.
* **Keep it Steady:** Leave your board flat on the table while it is plugged in. Jerking or pulling the cable while it is connected can loose the connection or damage the ports.

---

## Troubleshooting: "My board isn't connecting!"

If the power light doesn't turn on, or your computer doesn't recognize the board, don't worry! Try these quick fixes:
1. **Push it in:** Check if both ends of the USB cable are fully plugged into the board and the computer.
2. **Swap Ports:** Try plugging the cable into a different USB port on your computer.
3. **Restart:** Unplug the board, close your programming software, plug the board back in, and reopen the software.
