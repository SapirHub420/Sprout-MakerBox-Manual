## Key Features & Specifications

### ION32 Architecture Specs
* **Processor Core:** High-performance architecture optimized for speedy execution.
* **Operating Voltage:** 3.3V / 5V dual-rail logic.
* **Enhanced Memory Capacity:** Plenty of space for storing complex multi-threaded PictoBlox scripts and data logs.
* **Expanded I/O Array:** Generous digital and analog pin assignments to manage complex modular setups simultaneously.

## SprouT Base Board Custom Features

The SprouT Base Board bridges the gap between raw processing power and educational accessibility by introducing rugged, kid-friendly hardware features:

### 1. Plug-and-Play Module Capability
Forget dealing with complicated pinout maps or tiny breakout boards. The ION32 base board paths everything out to keyed, dedicated connectors.
* **Error-Free Wiring:** Eliminates loose wires and backwards component plugging, keeping students focused on creative logic instead of debugging hardware shorts.
* **Color-Coded Rows:** Groups inputs, outputs, and communication ports for easy visual tracking.

### 2. Dedicated High-Current Motor Driver Support
Unlike smaller microcontrollers, the ION32 Base Board features heavy-duty traces and specialized pin headers built specifically for running moving parts:
* **Servo & Actuator Rails:** Direct 3-pin configuration lines allow multiple heavy servos to drive smoothly without causing power drops to the central processor.
* **Robust Circuit Isolation:** Safeguards the main chip against reverse electromotive force (EMF) spikes common in motor-heavy robotics.

### 3. Versatile External Power Supply
Designed for untethered rover projects, smart greenhouse automation, and standalone builds that can run far away from a laptop USB port.
* **Wide Range DC Jack:** Accepts external battery packs or wall adapters ranging between **7V and 12V DC**.
* **Smart Voltage Regulation:** An efficient onboard power management IC seamlessly drops high incoming battery voltages down to safe, steady working rails.

## Safety Quick-Tip

> ### External Power Rule
> Always double-check your external battery packs to ensure they deliver between 7V and 12V DC. Never supply power beyond 12V to avoid overheating the onboard regulation circuits!
